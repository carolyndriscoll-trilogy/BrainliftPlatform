    - Source 1: Attachment Styles, Ephor
      - DOK 1 - Facts
        - There are two axes in attachment styles: anxiety and avoidance
          - Think of them as sliders
          - Anxiety = worry about people leaving
          - Avoidance = not talking about feelings
        - Style #1: Secure
          - Low anxiety, low avoidance
          - “I’m lovable and others are reliable.”
          - Patterns
            - Warm reciprocity, quick conflict repair
            - 
        - Style #2: Anxious-preoccupied
          - High anxiety, low avoidance
          - "I need people close, if there is any distance, it's a threat"
          - Patterns
            - Protest behaviors, needs constant reassurance
            - ↑ anxiety, panic, social‑media over‑engagement; higher inflammation
        - Style #3: Dismissive-avoidant
          - Low anxiety, high avoidance
          - “I’m safest solo because intimacy is risky.”
          - Patterns
            - Emotional distance, ghosting, disconnect under stress
            - ↑ substance use, lower help‑seeking, cardiovascular risk
        - 
          - High anxiety, high avoidance
          - "I want you, but you'll hurt me"
          - Patterns
            - Flip‑flop intimacy & withdrawal
            - Strongest link to PTSD, self‑harm, dissociation
            - Strongest link to feeling like they don't deserve love
        - Where do they come from? 
          - Early caregiver sensitivity → internal working models.
          - Later corrective (or compounding) experiences: partners, mentors, therapy.
          - Genes × environment: temperament, serotonin & oxytocin genes tweak thresholds but don’t lock you in.
          - Epigenetics & stress hormones: chronic threat exposure can bias the HPA axis toward hyper‑ or hypo‑arousal
          - Bad relationships
        - Ways to become secure
          - Emotionally Focused Therapy (EFT)

            - Stack self‑compassion: 5‑minute daily journaling (Three O’s: Observe feeling → Offer kindness → Opt for small need you can meet).
            - [https://www.simplypsychology.org/self-compassion-and-attachment-in-adults.html](https://www.simplypsychology.org/self-compassion-and-attachment-in-adults.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
          - Mindfulness + somatic work
            - Lowers attachment anxiety by reducing interoceptive threat misreads.
          - “Earned secure” relationships
            - One consistently responsive partner/friend can remodel internal models over a few years.
        - Self-audit
          - When I need help, do I reach out or power through alone?
          - When a text isn’t answered quickly, do I catastrophize or shrug?
          - Do compliments feel good or uncomfortable?
