FIRST: WHAT WENT WRONG (SO YOU DON’T FEEL CRAZY)

Replit didn’t “break” anything technically. It did three UX-hostile things:

It promoted filters to primary UI

“All”, “+ Add”, “Search” now compete with the content

It turned the list into a productivity dashboard

Progress counts, side filters, and actions all at once

It removed narrative flow

The list no longer tells a story or path

That’s why it feels worse.
Your intuition is 100 percent correct.

THE CORE PRINCIPLE (WRITE THIS DOWN)

This page is a reading syllabus, not a task manager.

Everything we do now follows from that.

THE FASTEST WAY TO FIX THIS PAGE (NO REWRITE)
STEP 1: KILL THE LEFT SIDEBAR FILTERS

This is the single most important change.

Remove or hide entirely:

“FACT CLUSTERS” sidebar

Counts (0/30, 0/2, etc.)

Progress bars

“Overall Progress”

Why:

Sidebars imply control and manipulation

This page is about consumption and reflection

Filters belong in advanced modes, not default view

If you do nothing else, do this.

STEP 2: REMOVE THE TOP-RIGHT CONTROLS FROM THIS PAGE

On the Reading List page only, remove or hide:

“All” dropdown

“+ Add”

“Search”

These belong on an admin or edit view, not a reader view.

Replace them with nothing.

Silence here is a feature.

STEP 3: TURN THE PAGE INTO A SINGLE NARRATIVE COLUMN

The Reading List should be one centered column, like a syllabus or reading memo.

Visually:

Max width

No side panels

No secondary rails

Just content flowing top to bottom

This immediately restores calm.

HOW TO STRUCTURE THE CONTENT (THIS IS THE FIX)
At the top of the Reading List tab, add exactly this:

Reading List
Sources selected to support, challenge, or contextualize the DOK1 fact base.
Read selectively. Grade alignment only after review.

That’s it. No timers. No promises. No productivity language.

GROUPING: DO NOT USE FILTERS, USE HEADERS

Instead of filters, use plain section headers.

Example:

Student Motivation and Interest

(Relates to Facts 1.1, 2.1)

(list of sources)

Explicit Instruction and Sentence-Level Writing

(Relates to Facts 1.2, 2.4)

(list of sources)

Cognitive Load and Writing-to-Learn

(Relates to Facts 3.1–4.2)

(list of sources)

Feedback, Mentorship, and Teacher Practice

(Relates to Facts 6.1–7.3)

(list of sources)

No collapses. No toggles. No filters.
This is best practice for deep-reading UIs in 2026.
